Model comparison — Anchor-first vs MRP-first

Same discount ladder and pretty-rounding. Different starting point.

Calculator Model comparison

Model A Anchor-first (current production model)

Start from 1-month headline anchor (incl. GST). Apply month-volume discount → headline. MRP is back-derived: MRP = headline × 4/3. Customer sees ~25% off (cosmetic). Real math = anchor × m × (1−d).

Model B MRP-first (proposed)

Start from 1-month MRP anchor. Apply month-volume discount → MRP at m months (ceil pretty-round). Headline = MRP × 0.75, then floor pretty-round → always ≤ 25% — i.e. student always gets ≥25% off. No "we promised 25, gave 24.9" complaints.

Shared rules across both models: Discount ladder (months 1–24): 0% · 2% · 4% · 6% · 8% · 10% · 12% · 15% · 17% · 20% · 22% · 24% · 26% · 28% · 30% · 32% · 34% · 36% · 38% · 40% · 42% · 44% · 46% · 48%. Pretty-round = ceil to next ₹50, minus ₹1 (always ends in 49 or 99). GST 18% inclusive in all headlines.

Why MRP-first differs: Two pretty-roundings stack — MRP rounds up (ceil ÷50 ×50 −1), headline rounds down (floor (n+1)÷50 ×50 −1). The asymmetric rounding guarantees real discount off MRP is always ≥25% — student never gets less than the advertised cut.
